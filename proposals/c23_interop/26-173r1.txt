Subject: Mark C_*_COMPLEX as obsolescent
Date: 2026-June-15

References: 26-007r1 26-172 25-205r1

In 26-007r1, the Editor identified unresolved technical issue (UTI) 003
"Redundant synonyms are unnecessary and unhelpful." In paper 26-172, the
HPC subgroup recommended (in agreement with the Editor) that the new
named constants of the form C_*_COMPLEX added by paper 25-205r1 be
removed. This paper proposes applying the same reasoning consistently to
existing C_*_COMPLEX named constants.

========

01. Background

In 26-007r1, the Editor introduced UTI 003:

      Redundant synonyms are unnecessary and unhelpful.

      Adding all those *_COMPLEX named constants just confuses people
      who haven't completely understood that type complex kind numbers
      are by definition identical to the real kind numbers for their
      parts. It bloats the language, the module, and the user programs
      that we thereby encourage to use these names. It's too late to get
      rid of the old ones, but we should not continue to add more
      useless and unnecessarily verbose names.

In 26-172, the HPC subgroup recommended reverting the addition of
*_COMPLEX named constants. Here, the subgroup recommends going further
and marking the other named constants as obsolescent, so as to actively
discourage new programs from using them.
